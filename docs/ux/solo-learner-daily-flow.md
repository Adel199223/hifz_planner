# Solo Learner Daily Flow

## Purpose

This file defines how the app should feel and behave for the solo learner.

Main UX goal:
**open app → understand next step immediately → finish a useful session with low friction**

---

## 1. Core UX rule

The learner should almost never be forced to build their own daily plan manually.

The app should behave more like:
- “Here is your next mission”

and less like:
- “Choose from many tools and screens”

---

## 2. New product center

The default landing experience should become:

# Today’s Hifz Path

This is the main home for the solo learner.

---

## 3. Recommended home screen structure

### Top section
- greeting or calm opener
- one-line status
  - “Your review is healthy today”
  - “Recovery mode: let’s stabilize first”
  - “New ayat unlocked after 2 reviews”

### Main primary card
- **Start Next Step**

This button launches the next mission immediately.

### Supporting cards
- Warm-up
- Due Review
- Weak Spots
- Similar Verses
- New Memorization
- Fluency

Not all cards need equal visual weight.
Only the next relevant action should dominate.

---

## 4. What the first screen should answer

The first screen should answer:

1. What should I do now?
2. How long will it take?
3. Is new memorization unlocked?
4. Am I in normal mode or recovery mode?

That is enough.
Do not overload the learner.

---

## 5. Recommended mission length

Default mission length:
- **3 to 7 minutes**

Longer work should be split into multiple missions, not one giant block.

Examples:
- 1-minute warm-up
- 4-minute due review
- 2-minute weak-spot repair
- optional 3-minute new memorization block

---

## 6. Recommended session order

### Standard day
1. Warm-up
2. Due review
3. Weak-spot repair if needed
4. New memorization if unlocked
5. Optional fluency

### Recovery day
1. Warm-up
2. Fragile recent review
3. Weak spots
4. Overdue review
5. No new memorization unless recovery clears

---

## 7. Screen behavior rules

### Rule 1
Always show one primary action.

### Rule 2
Keep secondary options visible but quiet.

### Rule 3
After each mission, show:
- what was completed
- what is next
- whether new memorization is now unlocked

### Rule 4
Use supportive language.
Never shame the learner for missing days.

---

## 8. Suggested textual wireframe

```text
[Header]
Assalamu alaykum
Balanced mode — 9 minutes suggested

[Primary card]
Next step: Due Review
4 ayat • about 4 min
[Start]

[Secondary cards]
Warm-up check        Ready
Weak spots           2 to repair
Similar verses       1 pair
New memorization     Locked until review is stable
Fluency              Optional
```

After the mission:

```text
Nice work.
You stabilized 3 fragile ayat.

Next best step:
Repair 1 weak transition
[Continue]
```

---

## 9. Variation to reduce boredom

Repetition is necessary, but the product should vary the *mode*, not the memory logic.

Good mission types:
- listen then recite
- read then hide then recite
- continue from a prompt
- repair the broken transition
- compare two similar verses
- uninterrupted recitation
- self-check with hint
- audio loop then free recall

This keeps the learner engaged without weakening the science.

---

## 10. New memorization UX

New memorization should feel:
- special
- small
- protected

Recommended behavior:
- clearly show when it is locked or unlocked
- explain why
- keep new chunks small
- require same-day lock-in before it feels “progressed”

Example wording:
- “New is available after you clear 2 fragile reviews”
- “New is paused today to protect what you already know”

---

## 11. Progress UX

Show honest progress, not just activity.

Recommended progress areas:
- stable coverage
- due today
- weak spots
- current streak
- current section / journey stage

Avoid overusing:
- total taps
- raw XP without meaning
- inflated levels disconnected from retention

---

## 12. Streak UX

Streaks should reward consistency, not perfection.

Recommended rule:
A day counts if the learner meaningfully engages with the planned path, including recovery work.

Avoid:
- “You lost everything”
- guilt-heavy language
- punishing users for life interruptions

Recommended recovery language:
- “You missed a few days. Let’s stabilize together.”
- “Recovery mode is active. No pressure — protect first.”

---

## 13. Messaging tone

The tone should be:
- respectful
- calm
- supportive
- spiritually appropriate
- never sarcastic
- never childish in a way that feels trivializing

This is not the same tone as a general gamified language app.

---

## 14. What to avoid

Avoid these UX mistakes:
- too many tabs fighting for attention
- dashboards that require interpretation before action
- dense blocks of instructions
- too many actions per screen
- long setup flows
- gamification noise before the queue is smart
- hiding core memorization behind secondary menus

---

## 15. Final UX summary

The solo learner should feel this:

> “The app knows where I am, it gives me one useful step at a time, and it helps me protect what I memorized.”

That is the right UX target.
