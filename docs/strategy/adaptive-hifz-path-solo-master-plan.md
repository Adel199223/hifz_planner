# Adaptive Hifz Path — Solo Learner Master Plan

## 1. Main goal

Turn Hifz Planner into a **daily adaptive Quran memorization companion** for a person learning alone.

The app should guide the learner from the beginning of their memorization journey to long-term maintenance after completion.

## 2. Primary user

### First target user
An adult or older teen learning Quran memorization **without depending on a teacher being present every day**.

### Important traits of the first target user
- often studies alone
- can get overwhelmed by too many options
- may have ADHD traits, dyslexia, or both
- needs clear next steps
- needs motivation without shame
- needs help balancing new memorization and revision
- may rely heavily on audio and repetition
- may miss days and need recovery without “starting over”

## 3. Product thesis

The winning product is **not** a literal Duolingo clone.

The winning product is:

**Duolingo-style guided UX + adaptive spaced review + Quran-native memorization structure**

In plain language:
- the app feels simple and guided like Duolingo
- the review timing learns from memory science like Anki/FSRS-style systems
- the actual flow respects Quran memorization realities like sabaq, sabqi, manzil, muraja’ah, tasmi’, and mutashabihat

## 4. Core product shift

### Old center of gravity
- reader
- planner
- disconnected companion features
- progress snapshots
- feature menus

### New center of gravity
- **Today’s Hifz Path**
- one obvious next action
- guided daily queue
- recovery when backlog grows
- visible mastery that reflects real retention, not just activity

## 5. North-star experience

When the solo learner opens the app, the app should answer this question immediately:

**“What is the best next memorization step for me right now?”**

The home screen should mainly show:

1. Warm-up check
2. Due review
3. Weak spots
4. Similar verses
5. New memorization (only if unlocked)
6. Optional fluency / tasmi’ check

## 6. The six jobs the app must do

A real hifz companion must manage all six of these jobs:

1. **New memorization**
2. **Same-day stabilization**
3. **Recent review**
4. **Old review / muraja’ah**
5. **Weak-spot repair**
6. **Similar-verse confusion training**

A seventh job becomes important later:
7. **Fluency / uninterrupted recitation / tasmi’**

## 7. Product principles

### Principle 1 — protect retention before adding novelty
The app must be willing to reduce or pause new memorization when review debt becomes too high.

### Principle 2 — one obvious next action
The learner should rarely face a “what should I do now?” decision.

### Principle 3 — short sessions by default
The app should work in tiny missions, especially for the solo learner.

### Principle 4 — the reader stays
The Quran reader is a strength and should remain the content engine.
Do not throw it away just to imitate Duolingo visually.

### Principle 5 — accessibility is part of the core product
ADHD and dyslexia support should influence the default experience, not be treated like optional extras.

### Principle 6 — progress should reflect stability, not tapping
Streaks and XP are allowed, but they must reflect retention and consistency.

### Principle 7 — preserve flexibility for future teacher mode
The first build is for solo learners, but the model should later support teacher-linked workflows without a total rewrite.

## 8. What the solo learner needs most

The solo learner needs the app to do five things extremely well:

1. decide what is due today
2. stop false progress
3. make review feel manageable
4. rescue weak areas quickly
5. keep motivation alive during long repetition-heavy periods

## 9. V1 boundaries

### What to build first
- guided daily path
- adaptive review queue
- manual self-grading
- new/review debt logic
- weak-spot queue
- accessibility defaults
- light gamification
- recovery mode after missed days

### What to delay
- rich teacher dashboards
- complicated social features
- competitive leaderboards
- heavy AI dependence for core scheduling
- advanced analytics that require major infrastructure
- over-designed avatar systems
- rewards that distract from memorization quality

## 10. What should stay from the current app foundation

Preserve and reuse these strengths:

- strong Quran reader
- audio and reciter controls
- local-first persistence
- settings infrastructure
- existing companion/session concepts where possible
- any existing route or data model that can be adapted instead of rewritten

## 11. What should change first

### The first visible change
The app’s “Today” experience should become the main guided memorization entry point.

### The first structural change
The existing companion/session logic should be wrapped inside a larger **Adaptive Hifz Path** system.

### The first behavioral change
New memorization should not always be available by default.
It should be unlocked or limited based on current review health.

## 12. What “gamification” should mean here

Gamification should mean:
- visible momentum
- small wins
- section / level feeling
- milestones
- gentle streaks
- repair quests
- review completion rewards

Gamification should **not** mean:
- shallow button pressing
- fake progress
- leaderboards as the main loop
- punishing shame systems
- rewarding quantity over stability

## 13. Success metrics for the first serious rebuild

The first rebuild should be judged by these outcomes:

### Daily behavior
- more users complete a meaningful daily session
- fewer users abandon after backlog builds up
- more users return after missing days

### Learning quality
- more due reviews completed on time
- fewer repeated lapses on the same units
- lower ratio of new memorization to neglected review
- stronger delayed recall

### User feeling
- less overwhelm
- more clarity
- more confidence
- less shame after interruptions

## 14. Non-negotiables

These are non-negotiable:

- keep the reader
- keep audio strong
- keep local-first behavior where possible
- optimize first for solo learners
- make the daily path the product center
- protect memorized material, not just forward movement
- build for real retention, not just surface engagement

## 15. Final summary

The app should stop behaving like a planner with memorization features.

It should become:

**a memorization operating system with a guided daily path, adaptive review, Quran-native revision structure, and accessible solo-first UX**
