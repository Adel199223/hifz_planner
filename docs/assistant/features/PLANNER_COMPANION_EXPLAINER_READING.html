Why it exists

The big picture

Takeaway Many memorization routines become stressful when new work grows faster than review quality can keep up.

When that happens, the review pile becomes heavier, the learner feels guilty, and the quality quietly drops.

This app is designed to reduce that problem by making the planner more cautious and the memorization flow more guided.

  • It tries to set a realistic load instead of an exciting load.
  • It tries to put the right task first instead of leaving you to guess.
  • It tries to check memory after delay, not only during the same sitting.

Quick recap The goal is not only to finish more. The goal is to keep what you finished.

Main parts

What each main part does

Takeaway Each part has one clear job, so you do not have to hold the whole system in your head all at once.

Plan

This is where you tell the app how much time you have, how many days you study, and how much new work is safe.

  • Benefit: it stops overloading you too early.
  • Benefit: it gives the planner a realistic daily budget.
  • Benefit: it lets you use revision-only days if you need them.

Today

This is your action screen. It tells you what to do now, in the order the app thinks protects retention best.

  • Benefit: less guessing.
  • Benefit: less decision fatigue.
  • Benefit: weaker due material can rise earlier.

Companion

This is the guided training area. It walks you through learning, recall, correction, and later stability checks.

  • Benefit: more guidance than a simple checklist.
  • Benefit: support falls away in layers instead of all at once.
  • Benefit: your results feed back into tomorrow's plan.

Quick recap Plan sets the rules. Today sets the order. Companion does the training.

Learning path

The Companion stages

Takeaway Companion is not one big test. It is a staged path where the help becomes lighter as your memory becomes stronger.

Stage 1: Acquisition

You get strong support at the start.

  • You hear and repeat.
  • You begin recall with more support.
  • Mistakes are corrected before they harden.

Stage 2: Bridge

The app starts pulling away some of the help.

  • You rely less on visible support.
  • You practice linking parts together.
  • You still get correction when needed.

Stage 3: Hidden recall

This is where the memory has to work with little or no help.

  • You recall more directly from memory.
  • Weak parts are targeted more carefully.
  • Correction still happens after failures.

Stage 4: Delayed consolidation

This is the later check, especially the next day.

  • The app checks whether memory survives time.
  • Due Stage-4 checks can soft-block new memorization.
  • This stage is there to catch false confidence.

Stage 5: Maintenance

This is the longer-term upkeep stage.

  • After a unit becomes stable, a later strong scheduled review can move it to maintained.
  • Weaker later reviews can lower it again.
  • That quality drop does not reopen Stage 4 automatically in the current version.

Meaning cues

If you need help, Companion can show a short meaning-based hint.

  • The cue comes from real translation data.
  • The source is shown clearly as a translation label.
  • If no good cue exists, the app falls back to normal hints.

Quick recap The stages exist so you do not have to jump from "brand new" to "perfect recall" in one leap.

Simple logic

How the app decides

Takeaway The algorithm is trying to protect memory quality first, then add new load only when it looks safe.

  1. Delayed checks come first because they test real stability after time has passed.
  2. Reviews usually come before new work because protecting old memorization matters.
  3. If two due items are similarly urgent, the weaker one can move earlier.
  4. If recent recall quality is weak, new work can shrink sooner and review gets more attention.
  5. Units can move through quality states such as Ready, Stable, and Maintained over time.
  6. Voice checking is not live yet, so you still self-grade after attempts for now.

One of the biggest upgrades so far is that the planner no longer looks only at what is due. It also reacts to how weak or strong recent Companion recall has been.

Quick recap This is no longer just a timer and a checklist. It is becoming a quality-aware memorization planner.

Current state

What is already built

Takeaway The planning and learning system is already real. The biggest missing piece is smarter automatic judging of recitation.

  • Real planning setup with time budgets, study days, revision-only days, and safe caps.
  • A Today flow that already orders delayed checks, review, and new work to protect retention first.
  • A full staged Companion flow for guided memorization, hidden-first recall, and delayed checks.
  • Meaning cues that come from real translation data when available.
  • Quality states such as Ready, Stable, and Maintained.
  • Planner reinforcement that already reacts to recent weakness and reduces new load sooner.

Quick recap What exists already is much more than a tracker. It is already a guided memorization system.

Next step

What comes next

Takeaway The next main upgrade is voice-based checking, while keeping manual fallback available.

In simple terms, the app should eventually listen to your recitation and help judge the attempt more automatically.

  • The app is already prepared for this in the background.
  • Manual fallback should still remain available.
  • The goal is smarter judging, not replacing the learning flow itself.

Quick recap What is missing is not the planner or the stage system. What is missing is better automatic evaluation.
