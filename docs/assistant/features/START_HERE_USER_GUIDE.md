# Start Here User Guide (First-Time, Non-Technical)

This is the first guide to open if you want to use the app without getting overwhelmed.

It is the shortest plain-language path to:
- knowing where to begin
- knowing which parts matter first
- knowing which advanced parts you can safely ignore for now

## Quick Start (No Technical Background)

1. Open `My Plan` and choose `Easy`, `Normal`, or `Intensive`.
2. Enter a realistic amount of study time, then press `Activate`.
3. Open `Today` and do the top task first.
4. Use `Practice from Memory` when the app tells you to practice, review, or do a delayed check.
5. Use `Read` when you want to understand a verse, then use `Study this verse` or `Save for later` if you want to come back to it.

## What This App Helps You Do

This app is built to help you:
- read Quran with meaning support
- memorize with a realistic daily plan
- protect old memorization instead of only chasing new pages
- come back to saved verses and notes later
- continue reading or studying without guessing where you left off

The app is trying to help you build steady long-term retention, not just finish more pages quickly.

## The Main Places You Need

- `Today`: the app’s coaching home. It tells you the safest next step right now.
- `My Plan`: where you set how strong or light your plan should be.
- `Practice from Memory`: where you actually do memorization practice, review practice, and delayed checks.
- `Read`: where you read Quran with translation, word help, transliteration, and verse study tools.
- `Library`: where you reopen verses and notes you saved for later.
- `My Quran`: your personal hub for continuing reading, reopening recent saved study items, checking your listening setup, and adjusting a few simple study defaults.

## How The Parts Work Together

The app works best in this order:

1. Set up a realistic plan in `My Plan`.
2. Follow the day’s priority in `Today`.
3. When `Today` sends you to `Practice from Memory`, do the practice there.
4. When you want understanding, go to `Read`.
5. If a verse matters and you want to return to it, use `Save for later` or write a note.
6. Reopen saved things from `Library`.
7. Use `My Quran` when you want one calm personal place to:
   - continue reading
   - reopen your latest saved study items
   - check your current reciter, repeat, and speed setup
   - switch simple study defaults like translation, word help, transliteration, or autoplay

What each main area is trying to achieve:

- `Today`: reduce decision fatigue and keep you on the safest next task.
- `My Plan`: stop you from choosing a pace that looks nice but is too heavy to keep.
- `Practice from Memory`: turn memorization into a guided session instead of a vague “study more” instruction.
- `Read`: help you understand what you are reading, not only view Arabic text.
- `Library`: make saved verses and notes useful later, not just stored somewhere.
- `My Quran`: give you one personal place to resume and reconnect with your current study setup without digging through deeper settings first.

## What To Ignore At First

You do not need these on day one:

- `Advanced` in `My Plan`
- `Calibration`
- `Forecast`
- `Quran Radio`
- deep settings that are only for fine-tuning

Safe beginner rule:
- start with `My Plan`, `Today`, `Practice from Memory`, `Read`, `Library`, and `My Quran`
- ignore the rest until the basic daily flow feels normal

## Common Situations

### I want to read and understand

1. Open `Read`.
2. Turn on translation, word help, or transliteration if needed.
3. Use `Study this verse` when one verse needs focused attention.
4. Use `Save for later` or a note if you want to revisit it.

### I want to memorize today

1. Open `Today`.
2. Do the top action first.
3. If it sends you to `Practice from Memory`, follow that path instead of guessing.

### I fell behind

1. Open `Today`.
2. If the app says `Protect retention` or `Recovery and stabilize`, follow that lighter guidance first.
3. If needed, go back to `My Plan` and reduce pressure instead of forcing more new work.

### I saved something and want to reopen it

1. Open `Library`.
2. Use `Saved verses` or `Notes`.
3. Press `Reopen in Reader` to go back to that verse.

### I want to continue where I left off

1. Open `My Quran`.
2. Use `Continue reading` if a recent reading target is available.
3. If there is no saved reading target yet, use `Open Reader`.
4. In the same screen, use the saved-study preview if you want to reopen your latest saved verse or note directly.
5. Use `Study setup` there if you want to quickly turn translation, word help, transliteration, or autoplay on or off.

## What This App Does Not Do Yet

Right now, the app does not try to do everything.

Important limits:
- it does not listen to your recitation and score it with a microphone
- it does not provide deep tafsir or a full explanation source inside the Reader
- `Quran Radio` is still not a finished daily-use feature
- some advanced planner tools are meant for later fine-tuning, not first-time setup
- the reading resume feature uses a practical verse/page target, not an exact scroll-position memory
