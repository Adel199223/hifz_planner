The big picture

What the app is really trying to do

A lot of memorization systems fail because they push new material too early, then the review pile grows, and the whole routine starts to feel heavy. This app is designed to reduce that problem.

The app's main goal Help you build memorization that still works after time passes, not just memorization that feels easy in the same sitting.

1. Keep the load realistic

The app asks how much time you really have and tries to fit your day around that instead of around wishful thinking.

2. Put the right task first

It shows delayed checks and reviews before most new work, because protecting old memorization matters.

3. Teach in stages

Companion does not expect perfect recall too early. It reduces help step by step.

4. Learn from your quality

If your recent recall looks weak, the planner gets more careful and reduces new pressure sooner.

Main areas

What each part of the app does for you

As a normal user, you mainly live in three places: Plan, Today, and Companion.

1

Plan

This is where you tell the app how much time you have, how many days you study, and how much new work is safe.

  • Benefit: stops overloading you too early.
  • Benefit: gives the planner a realistic daily budget.
  • Benefit: lets you use revision-only days if needed.
2

Today

This is your action screen. It tells you what to do now, in the order the app thinks protects your retention best.

  • Benefit: less guessing and less decision fatigue.
  • Benefit: due work is already prioritized for you.
  • Benefit: weak material can rise earlier in the list.
3

Companion

This is the guided training area. It walks you through learning, recalling, correcting mistakes, and later proving stability.

  • Benefit: more guided than a simple checklist.
  • Benefit: builds confidence in layers, not all at once.
  • Benefit: connects your actual performance back to the planner.
Simple daily loop You set the rules in Plan. You follow the order in Today. You do the actual memorization and review in Companion. Then the result feeds back into tomorrow's plan.
Companion explained

What the Companion stages mean in real life

Companion is not one single screen with one single rule. It is a staged path. The help becomes lighter as your memory becomes stronger.

Stage 1

Acquisition

This is the early learning stage. You get strong support. The goal is to start the memory correctly.

  • You hear and repeat.
  • You begin early recall, but with more support.
  • Mistakes are corrected before they harden.

Why it helps: good memorization usually begins with a clean start.

Stage 2

Bridge

The app starts to pull away some of the help. You are moving from supported recall to more independent recall.

  • You rely less on visible support.
  • You practice linking parts together.
  • You still get correction when needed.

Why it helps: it stops you from becoming dependent on clues.

Stage 3

Hidden recall

This is where the memory has to work with little or no help. The app tests whether recall is becoming real.

  • You recall from memory more directly.
  • Weak parts are targeted more carefully.
  • Correction still happens after failures.

Why it helps: this is where memorization becomes robust instead of fragile.

Stage 4

Delayed consolidation

This usually means a later check, especially the next day. The app wants to see whether the memory survives time.

  • The check happens after delay, not only right away.
  • Weak spots are tested first.
  • New work can be blocked if this part is due.

Why it helps: it catches fake confidence that only lasts inside the same session.

Stage 5

Maintenance

Once a unit has proved itself, it moves into long-term upkeep. It is still watched, but not treated like brand-new material forever.

  • Strong later reviews can keep it in a healthy state.
  • Weaker later reviews can lower its status again.
  • That quality change does not restart everything automatically.

Why it helps: strong material stays alive without wasting effort.

Extra help

Meaning cues and recitation support

If you need help, Companion can show a short meaning-based hint taken from translation data, with the source named.

  • This is not a random hint.
  • It comes from real verse translation data.
  • If no good cue exists, the app falls back to normal hints.

Why it helps: meaning can support recall without giving away the whole answer.

Simple algorithm view

How the app decides what comes first

You asked for the algorithm in simple words. Here is the human version.

Rule 1: Check delayed stability first

If a delayed check is due, that matters a lot. The app wants to know whether yesterday's work is still solid today.

Rule 2: Protect old memorization before adding more

Reviews usually come before new work. This is the app saying, "Do not build on a shaky base."

Rule 3: Weak recent performance gets more attention

If two review items are similarly urgent, the weaker one can move earlier. The planner now uses Companion quality to do that better.

Rule 4: Weak retention shrinks new work sooner

If recent recall quality is weak, the app becomes more conservative. It gives more of today's energy back to review.

Rule 5: Better quality changes the long-term status

Units can move through status levels like Ready, Stable, and Maintained depending on how they perform over time.

Rule 6: Voice checking is not live yet

Right now, you still self-grade after an attempt. The app is prepared for future voice evaluation, but it has not shipped yet.

The most important planner upgrade so far The app no longer looks only at "what is due." It also reacts to "how strong or weak your recent recall has been." That makes the planner much smarter than a simple calendar.
Concrete example

What one unit can look like across a few days

This example is not about every tiny detail. It is about the shape of the journey.

Day 1: You learn the unit

You open the new task from Today. Companion starts with stronger support, then gradually asks for more recall.

Later on Day 1: The help drops

As you continue, the app hides more, asks for stronger recall, and focuses on weak spots that need more work.

Next day: Delayed check

The app asks, "Is it still there now that time has passed?" If yes, the unit gets a stronger quality state. If no, the app knows it still needs reinforcement.

Later: Scheduled review

The unit returns in review. Strong results help it move into longer-term maintenance. Weak results can lower its status and demand more attention.

Long term: Maintenance

The best outcome is not just "I learned it once." The best outcome is "I can still retrieve it later with confidence."

What is already done

What has already been built in the Planner and Companion

This is the practical summary of what exists now, not the future wish list.

Real planning setup

You can set time budget, session structure, study days, revision-only days, and safe caps for new work.

Today execution flow

The app already orders delayed checks, reviews, and new work so that retention is protected first.

Full staged Companion flow

The app already supports guided new memorization, hidden-first review, and delayed Stage-4 checks.

Meaning cues

You can now get short meaning-based hints from real translation data, with the source shown clearly.

Status tracking

Units can now live in quality states like Ready, Stable, and Maintained instead of all being treated the same.

Planner reacts to weakness

The planner now uses recent Companion weakness to move fragile review items earlier and to shrink new work sooner when needed.

Review save-back is connected

Review results from Companion feed back into the shared planner path, so quality and scheduling stay connected.

Manual grading still works

Even though voice evaluation is not live yet, the whole learning flow still works today with manual correct/incorrect grading.

Why this matters The app is already far beyond a simple page tracker. It is a guided memorization system that tries to balance volume, review pressure, and long-term stability.
Next stages

The next main upgrade is voice-based checking. That means the app should eventually listen to your recitation and help judge your attempt more automatically.

Next big step

Voice evaluation

The app is already prepared for this in the background. The goal is to let Companion use speech evaluation while still keeping manual fallback.

Important note

Manual fallback should stay

Even when voice checking arrives, it is still wise to keep a manual fallback. Recitation checking is too important to trust blindly.

Short version: what is already built is the learning system itself. What comes next is making the judging step smarter and more automatic.
